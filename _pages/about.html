I am a Post Doctoral Research Associate in the Computer Science and Mathematics Division at the Oak Ridge National Laboratory. Broadly speaking, my research expertise and interests are in development of data analysis techniques.

I received a Ph.D. degree in the School of Electrical and Computer Engineering at Purdue University, where I worked with Professor David S. Ebert. I also received a B.S. in Computer Engineering and Electrical Engineering (Dual Major) from Kyung Hee University, South Korea. During my doctoral research, I have focused on the visual analytics approach integrating human reasoning, data mining, and statistical techniques for knowledge discovery in big data, such as textual and spatiotemporal information in a large volume of social media.
